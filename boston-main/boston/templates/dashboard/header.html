Chronic lung disease is a broad term for a number of diseases of the lung airways and other structures of the lung, including chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD). About 65 million people suffer from COPD and 3 million die from it each year, making it the third leading cause of death worldwide.


Pulmonary Drug discovery lab (lab1) is a partnership between Bayer, Partners HealthCare’s founding members Brigham and Women’s Hospital (BWH), and Massachusetts General Hospital (MGH). It is aimed at developing new drug candidates to treat chronic lung diseases.


The joint lab hosts scientists from all three parties (i.e. Bayer, BWH, MGH). Bayer is investing more than USD 30 million to fund joint research projects over the next five years (2019-2024).
